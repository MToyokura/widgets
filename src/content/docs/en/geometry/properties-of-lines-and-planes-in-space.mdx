## A line perpendicular to a plane

To say that a line is perpendicular to a plane, it is sufficient to show that it is perpendicular to two intersecting lines on that plane. Once this condition is met, the line is defined as perpendicular to the entire plane, and as a result, the angle it makes with every line existing on the plane is 90 degrees.
